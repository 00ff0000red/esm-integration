Values¶

Bytes¶

The simplest form of value are raw uninterpreted bytes. In the abstract syntax they are represented as hexadecimal literals.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{bytes} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}} &::=& \def\mathdef1225#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1225{00} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1226#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1226{FF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Conventions¶

  • The meta variable \(b\) range over bytes.
  • Bytes are sometimes interpreted as natural numbers \(n < 256\).

Integers¶

Different classes of integers with different value ranges are distinguished by their size and their signedness.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{unsigned integers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1193#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef1193{N}} &::=& 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^N{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{signed integers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1199#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef1199{N}} &::=& -2^{N-1} ~|~ \dots ~|~ {-}1 ~|~ 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^{N-1}{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{uninterpreted integers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1204#1{{\mathit{int}_{#1}}}\mathdef1204{N}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1193#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef1193{N}} ~|~ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1199#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef1199{N}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The latter class defines uninterpreted integers, whose signedness interpretation can vary depending on context. In those contexts, a conversion based on 2’s complement will be applied for values that are out-of-range for a chosen interpretation. That is, semantically, when interpreted as unsigned, negative values \(-n\) convert to \(2^N-n\), and when interpreted as signed, positive values \(n \geq 2^{N-1}\) convert to \(n-2^N\).

Conventions¶

  • The meta variables \(m, n, i\) range over integers.
  • Numbers may be denoted by simple arithmetics, as in the grammar above.

Floating-Point¶

Floating-point data consists of values in binary floating-point format according to the IEEE 754 standard.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{floating-point numbers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\def\mathdef1207#1{{\mathit{float}_{#1}}}\mathdef1207{N}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}}^{N/8} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The two possible sizes \(N\) are 32 and 64.

Vectors¶

Vectors are bounded sequences of the form \(A^n\) (or \(A^\ast\)), where the \(A\)-s can either be values or complex constructions. A vector can have at most \(2^{32}-1\) elements.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lllll} \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{vectors} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-vec}{\mathit{vec}}(A) &::=& A^n & (n < 2^{32})\\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Names¶

Names are sequences of scalar Unicode code points.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{names} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{name}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}}^\ast \\ \def\mathdef1192#1{{}}\mathdef1192{code points} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}} &::=& \def\mathdef1227#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1227{0000} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1228#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1228{D7FF} ~|~ \def\mathdef1229#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1229{E000} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1230#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1230{10FFFF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]
