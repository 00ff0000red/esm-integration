Values¶

Bytes¶

The simplest form of value are raw uninterpreted bytes. In the abstract syntax they are represented as hexadecimal literals.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef499#1{{}}\mathdef499{bytes} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}} &::=& \def\mathdef515#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\mathdef515{00} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef516#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\mathdef516{FF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Conventions¶

  • The meta variable \(b\) range over bytes.
  • The meta function \(\mathrm{byte}(n)\) denotes the byte representing the natural number \(n < 256\).

Integers¶

Different classes of integers with different value ranges are distinguished by their size and their signedness.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef499#1{{}}\mathdef499{unsigned integers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef500#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef500{N}} &::=& 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^N{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef499#1{{}}\mathdef499{signed integers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef506#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef506{N}} &::=& -2^{N-1} ~|~ \dots ~|~ {-}1 ~|~ 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^{N-1}{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef499#1{{}}\mathdef499{uninterpreted integers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef509#1{{\mathit{int}_{#1}}}\mathdef509{N}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef500#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef500{N}} ~|~ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef506#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef506{N}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The latter class defines uninterpreted integers, whose signedness interpretation can vary depending on context. In those contexts, a conversion based on 2’s complement will be applied for values that are out-of-range for a chosen interpretation. That is, semantically, when interpreted as unsigned, negative values \(-n\) convert to \(2^N-n\), and when interpreted as signed, positive values \(n \geq 2^{N-1}\) convert to \(n-2^N\).

Conventions¶

  • The meta variables \(m, n\) range over unsigned integers.
  • Numbers may be denoted by simple arithmetics, as in the grammar above.

Floating-Point¶

Floating-point data consists of values in binary floating-point format according to the IEEE 754 standard.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef499#1{{}}\mathdef499{floating-point numbers} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\def\mathdef512#1{{\mathit{float}_{#1}}}\mathdef512{N}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}}^{N/8} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The two possible sizes \(N\) are 32 and 64.

Vectors¶

Vectors are bounded sequences of the form \(A^n\) (or \(A^\ast\)), where the \(A\)-s can either be values or complex constructions. A vector can have at most \(2^{32}-1\) elements.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lllll} \def\mathdef499#1{{}}\mathdef499{vectors} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-vec}{\mathit{vec}}(A) &::=& A^n & (n < 2^{32})\\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Names¶

Names are vectors of bytes interpreted as character strings.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef499#1{{}}\mathdef499{names} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{name}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-vec}{\mathit{vec}}(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}}) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]
