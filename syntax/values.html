Values¶

Bytes¶

The simplest form of value are raw uninterpreted bytes. In the abstract syntax they are represented as hexadecimal literals.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{byte} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}} &::=& \def\mathdef1795#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1795{00} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1796#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1796{FF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Conventions¶

  • The meta variable \(b\) range over bytes.
  • Bytes are sometimes interpreted as natural numbers \(n < 256\).

Integers¶

Different classes of integers with different value ranges are distinguished by their size and their signedness.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{unsigned integer} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1748#1{{\mathit{u}#1}}\mathdef1748{N}} &::=& 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^N{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{signed integer} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1754#1{{\mathit{s}#1}}\mathdef1754{N}} &::=& -2^{N-1} ~|~ \dots ~|~ {-}1 ~|~ 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^{N-1}{-}1 \\ \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{uninterpreted integer} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1759#1{{\mathit{i}#1}}\mathdef1759{N}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef1748#1{{\mathit{u}#1}}\mathdef1748{N}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The latter class defines uninterpreted integers, whose signedness interpretation can vary depending on context. In the abstract syntax, they are represented as unsigned. However, some operations convert them to signed based on a 2’s complement interpretation.

Conventions¶

  • The meta variables \(m, n, i\) range over integers.
  • Numbers may be denoted by simple arithmetics, as in the grammar above.

Floating-Point¶

Floating-point data consists of values in binary floating-point format according to the IEEE 754 standard.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{floating-point number} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-float}{\def\mathdef1762#1{{\mathit{f}#1}}\mathdef1762{N}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-byte}{\mathit{byte}}^{N/8} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The two possible sizes \(N\) are 32 and 64.

Vectors¶

Vectors are bounded sequences of the form \(A^n\) (or \(A^\ast\)), where the \(A\)-s can either be values or complex constructions. A vector can have at most \(2^{32}-1\) elements.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lllll} \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{vector} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-vec}{\mathit{vec}}(A) &::=& A^n & (n < 2^{32})\\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Names¶

Names are sequences of scalar Unicode code points.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{name} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{name}} &::=& \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}}^\ast & (|\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}}^\ast)| < 2^{32}) \\ \def\mathdef1744#1{{}}\mathdef1744{code point} & \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-name}{\mathit{codepoint}} &::=& \def\mathdef1797#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1797{0000} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1798#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1798{D7FF} ~|~ \def\mathdef1799#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1799{E000} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef1800#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1800{10FFFF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Due to the limitations of the binary format, the lengths of a name is bounded by the length of its UTF-8 encoding. The auxiliary \(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}\) function expressing this encoding is defined as follows:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lcl@{\qquad}l} \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c^\ast) &=& (\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c))^\ast \\[1ex] \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b & (c < \def\mathdef1801#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1801{80} \wedge c = b) \\ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b_1~b_2 & (\def\mathdef1802#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1802{80} \leq c < \def\mathdef1803#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1803{800} \wedge c = 2^6\cdot(b_1-\def\mathdef1804#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1804{C0})+(b_2-\def\mathdef1805#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1805{80})) \\ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b_1~b_2~b_3 & (\def\mathdef1806#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1806{800} \leq c < \def\mathdef1807#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1807{10000} \wedge c = 2^{12}\cdot(b_1-\def\mathdef1808#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1808{C0})+2^6\cdot(b_2-\def\mathdef1809#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1809{80})+(b_3-\def\mathdef1810#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1810{80})) \\ \href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-utf8}{\mathrm{utf8}}(c) &=& b_1~b_2~b_3~b_4 & (\def\mathdef1811#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1811{10000} \leq c < \def\mathdef1812#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef1812{110000} \wedge c = 2^{18}\cdot(b_1-\def\mathdef1813#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1813{C0})+2^{12}\cdot(b_2-\def\mathdef1814#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1814{80})+2^6\cdot(b_3-\def\mathdef1815#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1815{80})+(b_4-\def\mathdef1816#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef1816{80})) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Convention¶

  • Code points are sometimes used interchangeably with natural numbers \(n < 1114112\).
