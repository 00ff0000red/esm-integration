Abstract Syntax¶

This specification defines WebAssembly as a programming language, in terms of an abstract syntax. For convenience, some semantic objects will also be defined syntactically.

Grammar¶

The following conventions are adopted in defining grammar rules.

  • Terminal symbols (atoms) are written in sans-serif: \(\mathsf{i32}, \mathsf{end}\).
  • Nonterminal symbols are written in italic: \(\mathit{valtype}, \mathit{instr}\).
  • An optional occurrence of \(x\) is written \(x^?\).
  • A possibly empty sequence of iterations of \(x\) is written \(x^\ast\).
  • A non-empty sequence of iterations of \(x\) is written \(x^+\).
  • A sequence of \(n\geq 0\) iterations of \(x\) is written \(x^n\).
  • The empty sequence is denoted by \(\epsilon\).
