Numbers¶

Integral Numbers¶

Different classes of integral numbers with different value ranges are distinguished by their size and their signedness. The following auxiliary syntactic classes are assumed:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(unsigned integers)} & {\mathit{uint}}_N &::=& 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^N{-}1 \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(signed integers)} & {\mathit{sint}}_N &::=& -2^{N-1} ~|~ \dots ~|~ {-}1 ~|~ 0 ~|~ 1 ~|~ \dots ~|~ 2^{N-1}{-}1 \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

When dealing with numbers, the following conventions are adopted:

  • The meta variables \(m, n, i, j, k\) range over integral numbers where the class is clear from context.
  • Numbers are sometimes denoted by simple arithmetics.

Binary Encoding¶

All integral numbers are encoded using the LEB128 variable-length integer encoding, in either unsigned or signed variant.

  • \({\mathit{uint}}_N\): unsigned integers are encoded in unsigned LEB128 format. As an additional constraint, the total number of bytes encoding a value of type \({\mathit{uint}}_N\) must not exceed \(\mathrm{ceil}(N/7)\) bytes.

    Formally:

    \[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{n}{{\mathit{uint}}_N} &=& \mathrm{byte}(n) & (n < 128) \\ \def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{m \cdot 128 + n}{{\mathit{uint}}_N} &=& \mathrm{byte}(n+128)~\def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{m}{{\mathit{uint}}_{N-7}} & (n < 128) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

    Note

    In the case of a value less than 128, both rules apply, allowing for optional padding bytes.

  • \({\mathit{sint}}_N\): signed integers are encoded in signed LEB128 format, which uses a 2’s complement representation. As an additional constraint, the total number of bytes encoding a value of type \({\mathit{sint}}_N\) must not exceed \(\mathrm{ceil}(N/7)\) bytes.

    Formally:

    \[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{n}{{\mathit{sint}}_N} &=& \mathrm{byte}(n) & (0 \leq n < 64) \\ \def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{-n}{{\mathit{sint}}_N} &=& \mathrm{byte}(128-n) & (0 < n \leq 64) \\ \def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{\pm m \cdot 128 + n}{{\mathit{sint}}_N} &=& \mathrm{byte}(n+128)~\def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{\pm m}{{\mathit{sint}}_{N-7}} & (n < 128) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

    Note

    In the case of a non-negative value less than 64, both the first and second rule apply, allowing for optional padding bytes. In the case of a negative value greater than or equal to -64, both the second and third rule apply, allowing for optional padding bytes.

Floating-point Numbers¶

All floating point numbers are represented as binary values according to the IEEE-754 standard.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(floating-point numbers)} & {\mathit{float}}_N &::=& b^{N/8} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The two possible sizes for \(N\) are 32 and 64.

When dealing with numbers, the following convention is adopted:

  • The variable \(z\) ranges over floating point values where the class is clear from context.

Binary Encoding¶

Floating point values are encoded directly by their IEEE bit pattern in little endian byte order:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\encodeinternal#1#2{\mathrm{encode}_{#2}(#1)}\encodeinternal{z}{{\mathit{float}}_N} &=& z \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Indices¶

Definitions in a module or function are referenced by index. All indices are encoded as \({\mathit{uint}_{32}}\) numbers.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(type indices)} & \mathit{typeindex} &::=& {\mathit{uint}_{32}} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(function indices)} & \mathit{funcindex} &::=& {\mathit{uint}_{32}} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(table indices)} & \mathit{tableindex} &::=& {\mathit{uint}_{32}} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(memory indices)} & \mathit{memindex} &::=& {\mathit{uint}_{32}} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(global indices)} & \mathit{globalindex} &::=& {\mathit{uint}_{32}} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(local indices)} & \mathit{localindex} &::=& {\mathit{uint}_{32}} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(label indices)} & \mathit{labelindex} &::=& {\mathit{uint}_{32}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]
