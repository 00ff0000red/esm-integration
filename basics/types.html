Types¶

Value Types¶

Value types classify the types of individual values. They are defined by the following grammar:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(value types)} & \mathit{valtype} &::=& \mathsf{i32} ~|~ \mathsf{i64} ~|~ \mathsf{f32} ~|~ \mathsf{f64} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The types \({\mathit{int}_{32}}\) and \({\mathit{int}_{64}}\) classify 32 and 64 bit integers, respectively. Integers are not inherently signed or unsigned, their interpretation is determined by individual operations.

The types \({\mathit{float}_{32}}\) and \({\mathit{float}_{64}}\) classify 32 and 64 bit floating points, respectively. They correspond to single and double precision floating point types as defined by the IEEE-754 standard

Covnentions¶

  • The meta variable \(t\) ranges over value types where clear from context.

Binary Encoding¶

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \enc{\mathsf{i32}}{\mathit{valtype}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{7F} \\ \enc{\mathsf{i64}}{\mathit{valtype}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{7E} \\ \enc{\mathsf{f32}}{\mathit{valtype}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{7D} \\ \enc{\mathsf{f64}}{\mathit{valtype}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{7C} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Note

These bytes correspond to the encodings of small negative |sint| values. This scheme is so that types can coexist in a single space with (positive) indices into the type section, which may be relevant for future extensions Gaps in this scheme are also reserved for future extensions.

Result Types¶

Result types classify the result values of functions or blocks. They are defined by the following grammar:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(result types)} & \mathit{resulttype} &::=& \mathit{valtype}^? \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Note

In the current version of WebAssembly, at most one value is allowed as a result. However, this may be generalized to multiple values in future versions.

Function Types¶

Function types classify the signature of functions. They are defined by the following grammar:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(function types)} & \mathit{functype} &::=& \mathit{valtype}^\ast \to \mathit{resulttype} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Binary Encoding¶

Function types are encoded by the byte \(\def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{60}\) followed by the vector of parameter types and the vector of result types.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \enc{t_1^\ast \to t_2^?}{\mathit{functype}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{60}~\enc{t_1^\ast}{}~\enc{t_2^?}{} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Table Types¶

Table types classify tables over elements classified by element types. They are defined by the following grammar:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(table types)} & \mathit{tabletype} &::=& \mathit{elemtype}[\mathit{limits}] \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(element types)} & \mathit{elemtype} &::=& \mathsf{anyfunc} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(limits)} & \mathit{limits} &::=& \uint32~\uint32^? \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

The limits constrain the minimum and optional maximum size of a table. If no maximum is given, the table can grow to any size.

Note

In future versions of WebAssembly, additional element types may be introduced.

Binary Encoding¶

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \enc{\mathsf{anyfunc}}{\mathit{elemtype}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{70} \\ \enc{t[n~m^?]}{\mathit{tabletype}} &=& \enc{t}{\mathit{elemtype}}~\enc{n~m^?}{\mathit{limits}} \\ ~ \\ \enc{n}{\mathit{limits}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{00}~\enc{n}{\uint32} \\ \enc{n~m}{\mathit{limits}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{01}~\enc{n}{\uint32}~\enc{m}{\uint32} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Memory Types¶

Memory types classify linear memories. They are defined by the following grammar:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(memory types)} & \mathit{memtype} &::=& \mathsf{page}[\mathit{limits}] \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Like tables, memories are constrained by limits for the minimum and optional maximum size. Both are given in units of page size.

Binary Encoding¶

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \enc{\mathsf{page}[n~m^?]}{\mathit{memtype}} &=& \enc{n~m^?}{\mathit{limits}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Global Types¶

Global types classify global variables and consist. They are defined by the following grammar:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(global types)} & \mathit{globaltype} &::=& \mathit{mutability}~\mathit{valtype} \\ \def\void#1{}\void{(mutability)} & \mathit{mutability} &::=& \mathsf{mut}^? \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Binary Encoding¶

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{lll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \enc{\mathsf{mut}^?~t}{\mathit{globaltype}} &=& \enc{t}{\mathit{valtype}}~\enc{\mathsf{mut}^?}{\mathit{mutability}} \\ \enc{\epsilon}{\mathit{mutability}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{00} \\ \enc{\mathsf{mut}}{\mathit{mutability}} &=& \def\hex#1{\mathrm{0x#1}}\hex{01} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

External Types¶

External types classify imports and exports. They are defined by the following grammar:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\void#1{}\void{(external types)} & \mathit{externtype} &::=& \mathsf{func}~\mathit{functype} ~|~ \mathsf{table}~\mathit{tabletype} ~|~ \mathsf{memory}~\mathit{memtype} ~|~ \mathsf{global}~\mathit{globaltype} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]
