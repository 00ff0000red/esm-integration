Instructions¶

Instructions are encoded by opcodes. Each opcode is represented by a single byte, and is followed by the instruction’s immediate arguments, where present. The only exception are structured control instructions, which consist of several opcodes bracketing their nested instruction sequences.

Note

Gaps in the byte code ranges encoding instructions are reserved for future extensions.

Control Instructions¶

Control instructions have varying encodings. For structured instructions, the nested instruction sequences are terminated with explicit opcodes for \(\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{end}}\) and \(\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{else}}\).

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef210#1{{}}\mathdef210{instructions} & \href{../binary/instructions.html#binary-instr}{\mathtt{instr}} &::=& \def\mathdef243#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef243{00} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{unreachable}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef244#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef244{01} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{nop}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef245#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef245{02}~~\mathit{rt}{:}\href{../binary/types.html#binary-resulttype}{\mathtt{resulttype}}~~(\mathit{in}{:}\href{../binary/instructions.html#binary-instr}{\mathtt{instr}})^\ast~~\def\mathdef246#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef246{0B} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{block}}~\mathit{rt}~\mathit{in}^\ast~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{end}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef247#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef247{03}~~\mathit{rt}{:}\href{../binary/types.html#binary-resulttype}{\mathtt{resulttype}}~~(\mathit{in}{:}\href{../binary/instructions.html#binary-instr}{\mathtt{instr}})^\ast~~\def\mathdef248#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef248{0B} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{loop}}~\mathit{rt}~\mathit{in}^\ast~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{end}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef249#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef249{04}~~\mathit{rt}{:}\href{../binary/types.html#binary-resulttype}{\mathtt{resulttype}}~~(\mathit{in}{:}\href{../binary/instructions.html#binary-instr}{\mathtt{instr}})^\ast~~\def\mathdef250#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef250{0B} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{if}}~\mathit{rt}~\mathit{in}^\ast~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{else}}~\epsilon~\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{end}} \def\mathdef251#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef251{04}~~\mathit{rt}{:}\href{../binary/types.html#binary-resulttype}{\mathtt{resulttype}}~~(\mathit{in}_1{:}\href{../binary/instructions.html#binary-instr}{\mathtt{instr}})^\ast~~ Note

The \(\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{else}}\) opcode \(\def\mathdef260#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef260{05}\) in the encoding of an \(\href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-control}{\mathsf{if}}\) instruction can be omitted if the following instruction sequence is empty.

Parametric Instructions¶

Parametric instructions are represented by single byte codes.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef210#1{{}}\mathdef210{instructions} & \href{../binary/instructions.html#binary-instr}{\mathtt{instr}} &::=& \dots \\ &&|& \def\mathdef261#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef261{1A} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-parametric}{\mathsf{drop}} \\ &&|& \def\mathdef262#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef262{1B} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-parametric}{\mathsf{select}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Variable Instructions¶

Variable instructions are represented by byte codes followed by the encoding of the respective index.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef210#1{{}}\mathdef210{instructions} & \href{../binary/instructions.html#binary-instr}{\mathtt{instr}} &::=& \dots \\ &&|& \def\mathdef263#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef263{20}~~x{:}\href{../binary/modules.html#binary-localidx}{\mathtt{localidx}} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-variable}{\mathsf{get\_local}}~x \\ &&|& \def\mathdef264#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef264{21}~~x{:}\href{../binary/modules.html#binary-localidx}{\mathtt{localidx}} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-variable}{\mathsf{set\_local}}~x \\ &&|& \def\mathdef265#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef265{22}~~x{:}\href{../binary/modules.html#binary-localidx}{\mathtt{localidx}} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-variable}{\mathsf{tee\_local}}~x \\ &&|& \def\mathdef266#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef266{23}~~x{:}\href{../binary/modules.html#binary-globalidx}{\mathtt{globalidx}} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-variable}{\mathsf{get\_global}}~x \\ &&|& \def\mathdef267#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef267{24}~~x{:}\href{../binary/modules.html#binary-globalidx}{\mathtt{globalidx}} &\Rightarrow& \href{../syntax/instructions.html#syntax-instr-variable}{\mathsf{set\_global}}~x \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]
