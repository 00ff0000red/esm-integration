Values¶

Bytes¶

Bytes encode by themselves.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll} \def\mathdef45#1{{}}\mathdef45{bytes} & \href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}} &::=& \def\mathdef78#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef78{00} &\Rightarrow& \def\mathdef79#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef79{00} \\ &&|&& \dots \\ &&|& \def\mathdef80#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef80{FF} &\Rightarrow& \def\mathdef81#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef81{FF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Integers¶

All integers are encoded using the LEB128 variable-length integer encoding, in either unsigned or signed variant.

Unsigned integers are encoded in unsigned LEB128 format. As an additional constraint, the total number of bytes encoding a value of type \(\def\mathdef82#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef82{N}\) must not exceed \(\mathrm{ceil}(N/7)\) bytes.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\mathdef45#1{{}}\mathdef45{unsigned integers} & \def\mathdef83#1{{\mathtt{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef83{N} &::=& n{:}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}} &\Rightarrow& n & (n < 128 \wedge n < 2^N) \\ &&|& n{:}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}}~~m{:}\def\mathdef84#1{{\mathtt{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef84{N-7} &\Rightarrow& 128\cdot m + (n-128) & (n \geq 128 \wedge N > 7) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Signed integers are encoded in signed LEB128 format, which uses a 2’s complement representation. As an additional constraint, the total number of bytes encoding a value of type \(\def\mathdef85#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef85{N}\) must not exceed \(\mathrm{ceil}(N/7)\) bytes.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\mathdef45#1{{}}\mathdef45{signed integers} & \def\mathdef86#1{{\mathtt{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef86{N} &::=& n{:}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}} &\Rightarrow& n & (n < 64 \wedge n < 2^{N-1}) \\ &&|& n{:}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}} &\Rightarrow& n-128 & (64 \leq n < 128 \wedge n \geq 128-2^{N-1}) \\ &&|& n{:}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}}~~m{:}\def\mathdef87#1{{\mathtt{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef87{N-7} &\Rightarrow& 128\cdot m + (n-128) & (n \geq 128 \wedge N > 7) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Uninterpreted integers are always encoded as signed integers.

\[\begin{array}{llcll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\mathdef45#1{{}}\mathdef45{uninterpreted integers} & \def\mathdef88#1{{\mathtt{int}_{#1}}}\mathdef88{N} &::=& n{:}\def\mathdef89#1{{\mathtt{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef89{N} &\Rightarrow& n \end{array}\]

Note

While the side conditions \(N > 7\) in the productions for non-terminating bytes restrict the length of the \(\def\mathdef90#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef90{}\) and \(\def\mathdef91#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef91{}\) encodings, “trailing zeros” are still allowed within these bounds. For example, \(\def\mathdef92#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef92{03}\) and \(\def\mathdef93#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef93{83}~\def\mathdef94#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef94{00}\) are both well-formed encodings for the value \(3\) as a \(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef48#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef48{8}}\). Similarly, either of \(\def\mathdef95#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef95{7e}\) and \(\def\mathdef96#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef96{FE}~\def\mathdef97#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef97{7F}\) and \(\def\mathdef98#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef98{FE}~\def\mathdef99#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef99{FF}~\def\mathdef100#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef100{7F}\) are well-formed encodings of the value \(-2\) as a \(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef54#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef54{16}}\).

The side conditions on the value \(n\) of terminating bytes further enforce that any unused bits in these bytes must be \(0\) for positive values and \(1\) for negative ones. For example, \(\def\mathdef101#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef101{83}~\def\mathdef102#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef102{10}\) is malformed as a \(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef48#1{{\mathit{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef48{8}}\) encoding. Similarly, both \(\def\mathdef103#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef103{83}~\def\mathdef104#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef104{3E}\) and \(\def\mathdef105#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef105{FF}~\def\mathdef106#1{\mathtt{0x#1}}\mathdef106{7B}\) are malformed as \(\href{../syntax/values.html#syntax-int}{\def\mathdef53#1{{\mathit{sint}_{#1}}}\mathdef53{8}}\) encodings.

Floating-Point¶

Floating point values are encoded directly by their IEEE bit pattern in little endian byte order:

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\mathdef45#1{{}}\mathdef45{floating-point numbers} & \def\mathdef107#1{{\mathtt{float}_{#1}}}\mathdef107{N} &::=& b^\ast{:\,}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}}^{N/8} &\Rightarrow& \mathrm{reverse}(b^\ast) \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Here, \(\mathrm{reverse}(b^\ast)\) denotes the byte sequence \(b^\ast\) in reversed order.

Vectors¶

Vectors are encoded with their length followed by the encoding of their element sequence.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\mathdef45#1{{}}\mathdef45{vectors} & \href{../binary/values.html#binary-vec}{\mathtt{vec}}(\mathtt{B}) &::=& n{:}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-int}{\def\mathdef67#1{{\mathtt{uint}_{#1}}}\mathdef67{32}}~~(x{:}\mathtt{B})^n &\Rightarrow& x^n \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Names¶

Names are encoded directly as a vector of bytes.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llcll} \def\mathdef45#1{{}}\mathdef45{names} & \href{../binary/values.html#binary-name}{\mathtt{name}} &::=& b^\ast{:\,}\href{../binary/values.html#binary-vec}{\mathtt{vec}}(\href{../binary/values.html#binary-byte}{\mathtt{byte}}) &\Rightarrow& b^\ast \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]
