Lexical Format¶

Characters¶

The text format assigns meaning to source text, which consists of a sequence of characters. Characters are assumed to be represented as valid Unicode code points.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llll} \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{character} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-char}{\mathtt{char}} &::=& \def\mathdef2235#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2235{00} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef2236#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2236{D800} ~|~ \def\mathdef2237#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2237{E000} ~|~ \dots ~|~ \def\mathdef2238#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2238{10FFFF} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Note

The set of characters interpreted in the text format is a subset of the characters supported by 7-bit ASCII, but a source text may contain other characters in comments or string literals.

Tokens¶

The character stream in the source text is divided, from left to right, into a sequence of tokens. In this specification, tokens are either defined explicitly by lexical grammar rules, such as the lexical syntax of values, or implicitly, by the occurrence of terminal symbols in literal form in the non-lexical grammar.

Tokens are formed from the input character stream according to the longest match rule. That is, the next token always consists of the longest possible sequence of characters that is recognized as a token.

Tokens may explicitly be separated by space, formatting characters or comments, collectively called white space. Except for string literals, white space cannot appear inside a token.

White Space¶

White space is any sequence of literal space characters, formatting characters, or comments. The allowed formatting characters correspond to a subset of the ASCII format effectors, namely, horizontal tabulation (\(\def\mathdef2239#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2239{09}\)), line feed (\(\def\mathdef2240#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2240{0A}\)), and carriage return (\(\def\mathdef2241#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2241{0D}\)).

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{white space} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-space}{\mathtt{space}} &::=& (\def\mathdef2242#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2242{~~} ~|~ \href{../text/lexical.html#text-format}{\mathtt{format}} ~|~ \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{comment}})^\ast \\ \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{format} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-format}{\mathtt{format}} &::=& \def\mathdef2243#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2243{09} ~|~ \def\mathdef2244#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2244{0A} ~|~ \def\mathdef2245#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2245{0D} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Comments¶

A comment can either be a line comment, started with a double semicolon \(\def\mathdef2187#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2187{{;}{;}}\) and extending to the end of the line, or a block comment, enclosed in delimiters \(\def\mathdef2185#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2185{{(}{;}} \dots \def\mathdef2186#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2186{{;}{)}}\). Block comments can be nested.

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{llclll@{\qquad\qquad}l} \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{comment} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{comment}} &::=& \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{linecomment}} ~|~ \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{blockcomment}} \\ \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{line comment} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{linecomment}} &::=& \def\mathdef2187#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2187{{;}{;}}~~\href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{linechar}}^\ast~~(\def\mathdef2246#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2246{0A} ~|~ \mathtt{eof}) \\ \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{line character} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{linechar}} &::=& c{:}\href{../text/lexical.html#text-char}{\mathtt{char}} & (c \neq \def\mathdef2247#1{\mathrm{U{+}#1}}\mathdef2247{0A}) \\ \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{block comment} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{blockcomment}} &::=& \def\mathdef2185#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2185{{(}{;}}~~\href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{blockchar}}^\ast~~\def\mathdef2186#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2186{{;}{)}} \\ \def\mathdef2184#1{{}}\mathdef2184{block character} & \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{blockchar}} &::=& c{:}\href{../text/lexical.html#text-char}{\mathtt{char}} & (c \neq \def\mathdef2248#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2248{;} \wedge c \neq \def\mathdef2249#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2249{(}) \\ &&|& \def\mathdef2250#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2250{;} & (\mbox{the next character is not}~\def\mathdef2251#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2251{)}) \\ &&|& \def\mathdef2252#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2252{(} & (\mbox{the next character is not}~\def\mathdef2253#1{\mbox{‘}\mathtt{#1}\mbox{’}}\mathdef2253{;}) \\ &&|& \href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{blockcomment}} \\ \end{array}\end{split}\]

Here, the pseudo token \(\mathtt{eof}\) indicates the end of the input. The look-ahead restrictions on the productions for \(\href{../text/lexical.html#text-comment}{\mathtt{blockchar}}\) disambiguate the grammar such that only well-bracketed uses of block comment delimiters are allowed.

Note

Any formatting and control characters are allowed inside comments.
